Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 15, 2023

Introduction

Welcome to Eco-nestro's Privacy Policy. This document outlines how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or use our services. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal data in compliance with applicable data protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).

By accessing or using our services, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our services.

This policy applies to all information collected through our website, mobile applications, and any related services, sales, marketing, or events. Please read this privacy policy carefully as it will help you make informed decisions about sharing your personal information with us.

Information We Collect

We collect various types of information to provide and improve our services to you. This includes:

1. Personal Identification Information

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when registering for an account, expressing an interest in obtaining information about us or our services, or otherwise contacting us. This may include:

  • Name and contact details (email address, phone number, mailing address)
  • Account credentials (username, password)
  • Payment information (credit card details, billing address)
  • Demographic information (age, gender, preferences)
  • Any other personal information you choose to provide

2. Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our website, we automatically collect certain information about your device and browsing behavior through cookies and similar technologies:

  • IP address and device identifiers
  • Browser type and version
  • Pages viewed and time spent on pages
  • Referring website addresses
  • Operating system and platform

3. Service Usage Data

We collect information about how you interact with our services, including:

  • Service access times and duration
  • Features used and preferences
  • Performance data and error reports
  • Search queries and results

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for various business purposes, including:

4. Service Provision and Account Management

We process your information to provide the services you request, including creating and managing your account, processing payments, scheduling services, and communicating with you about your account. This includes sending service-related emails, notifications, and administrative messages essential to your use of our services.

5. Personalization and Improvement

We analyze user behavior to personalize your experience, recommend relevant services, and improve our website functionality. This includes remembering your preferences, suggesting gardening services based on your location and past interactions, and optimizing our website layout based on user navigation patterns.

6. Marketing and Promotions

With your consent, we may send you promotional communications about our services, special offers, and gardening tips. You can opt-out of these communications at any time. We may also use your information to display targeted advertisements on third-party platforms based on your interests and browsing behavior.

7. Legal Compliance and Security

We use your information to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, enforce our agreements, and protect against fraudulent or illegal activities. This includes verifying identities for fraud prevention, investigating security incidents, and responding to lawful requests from public authorities.

8. Analytics and Research

We analyze aggregated data to understand user trends, measure service effectiveness, and conduct market research. This helps us develop new features, improve existing services, and make informed business decisions. All analytics are performed on anonymized or pseudonymized data where possible.

Information Sharing and Disclosure

We may share your information in the following circumstances:

9. Service Providers

We engage trusted third-party companies to facilitate our services, including payment processors, hosting providers, marketing platforms, and analytics services. These providers have access to your information only to perform specific tasks on our behalf and are obligated not to disclose or use it for other purposes.

10. Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, reorganization, bankruptcy, or similar corporate transaction, we may transfer your information as part of the business assets. You will be notified via email and/or prominent notice on our website of any change in ownership or uses of your personal information.

11. Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities (e.g., court orders, subpoenas). This includes situations where disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, your safety, or the safety of others, investigate fraud, or respond to government requests.

12. With Your Consent

We may share your information with third parties when we have your explicit consent to do so. For example, if you choose to participate in a co-marketing promotion with one of our partners, we may share relevant information with that partner to facilitate the offer.

Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information:

13. Encryption and Access Controls

We use SSL/TLS encryption for data transmission and store sensitive information using industry-standard encryption methods. Access to personal data is restricted to authorized personnel who need the information to perform their job functions, and all access is logged and monitored.

14. Security Practices

We maintain comprehensive security policies, conduct regular security audits, and provide employee training on data protection. Our systems are protected by firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and regular vulnerability scanning to identify and address potential security risks.

15. Incident Response

We have established procedures to handle potential data breaches, including notification processes to comply with applicable laws. In the event of a security incident that affects your personal data, we will promptly investigate the issue and notify you and relevant authorities as required by law.

16. Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. When determining retention periods, we consider the amount, nature, and sensitivity of the data, potential risk from unauthorized use, and legal requirements.

Your Data Protection Rights

Depending on your location, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information:

17. Access and Portability

You may request a copy of your personal data in a structured, commonly used format. We will provide this information within a reasonable timeframe, typically within 30 days of receiving your request. You can access much of your information directly through your account settings.

18. Correction

You may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data. We encourage you to review and update your account information regularly to ensure it remains accurate. You can update most of your personal information through your account settings or by contacting our support team.

19. Deletion

You may request deletion of your personal data, subject to certain legal exceptions. Upon receiving a valid deletion request, we will remove your personal data from our active systems, though some information may remain in backup archives for legal compliance purposes until those backups are cycled out.

20. Restriction and Objection

You may request restriction of processing or object to certain types of processing, such as direct marketing. If you object to processing based on legitimate interests, we will reassess the balance between our interests and your rights. You can manage many of these preferences through your account settings.

21. Withdraw Consent

Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal. This includes opting out of marketing communications, which you can do by clicking the unsubscribe link in any marketing email or adjusting your account preferences.

22. Non-Discrimination

We will not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights. This means we will not deny you services, charge different prices, or provide a different level or quality of services because you exercised any of your privacy rights, unless permitted by applicable laws.

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience:

23. Types of Cookies

We use several types of cookies, including essential cookies for website functionality, performance cookies to analyze site usage, functionality cookies to remember your preferences, and targeting cookies for advertising purposes. Each cookie serves a specific purpose in improving your experience with our services.

24. Cookie Management

You can control cookies through your browser settings and our cookie consent banner. Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies or alert you when cookies are being sent. However, disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website and services. Our cookie banner provides granular control over different cookie categories.

25. Do Not Track Signals

Our website does not currently respond to Do Not Track (DNT) browser signals. However, we provide equivalent privacy controls through our cookie management tools and privacy settings. We are monitoring developments around DNT signals and may implement support in the future as standards evolve.

26. Third-Party Tracking

We allow certain third parties to use tracking technologies on our website for analytics and advertising purposes. These third parties may collect information about your online activities over time and across different websites. We carefully vet these partners and require them to adhere to strict data protection standards.

International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your own:

27. Transfer Mechanisms

When transferring data outside the European Economic Area (EEA), we use appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission. We ensure that all international data transfers comply with applicable data protection laws and provide adequate protection for your personal information.

28. Data Localization

We store data primarily in the United States but may use cloud services with global infrastructure. Regardless of where your data is processed, we apply the same protections described in this policy. We take steps to ensure that our service providers maintain adequate security measures regardless of location.

Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to children under the age of 16:

29. Age Restrictions

We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 16 without parental consent. If we become aware that we have collected personal information from a child without verification of parental consent, we will take steps to remove that information from our servers. Parents or guardians who believe their child has provided us with personal information should contact us immediately.

Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites:

30. External Sites

We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of external sites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit. These third-party sites may independently collect data, place cookies, or solicit personal information from you, and our privacy policy does not cover their practices.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time:

31. Notification of Changes

We will notify you of significant changes through email or prominent website notices. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy indicates when revisions were made. We encourage you to periodically review this page for the latest information on our privacy practices. Your continued use of our services after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Contact Information

If you have questions about this privacy policy:

32. Data Controller

Eco-nestro LLC is the data controller for personal information collected through our services. Our registered office is located at 80 Lafayette St, New York, NY 10013, USA. You can contact our Data Protection Officer for privacy-related inquiries.

33. Contact Methods

You may contact us at privacy@eco-nestro.com or by mail at the address above. For general inquiries, please use our main contact form. For privacy-specific concerns, please include "Privacy Request" in your subject line to ensure prompt handling by our privacy team.

34. Response Time

We strive to respond to all privacy-related inquiries within 30 days. For complex requests, we may need additional time and will notify you of any delay. Please provide sufficient detail in your request to help us process it efficiently, including your account information if applicable.

Additional Provisions

35. California Privacy Rights

California residents have specific rights under the CCPA, including the right to request disclosure of data collection and sharing practices, and to opt-out of the sale of personal information. California consumers may request this information twice per calendar year. We do not sell personal information as defined by the CCPA.

36. Nevada Privacy Rights

Nevada residents may opt-out of the sale of certain personal information under Nevada law. While we do not currently sell personal information as defined by Nevada law, you may submit an opt-out request which we will honor if our practices change in the future.

37. EU/UK GDPR Rights

EU and UK residents have additional rights under the GDPR, including the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. If you believe our processing of your personal data infringes data protection laws, you have the right to file a complaint with the relevant data protection authority in your country of residence.

38. Canadian Privacy Rights

Canadian residents have rights under PIPEDA, including the right to access and challenge the accuracy of their personal information. We are committed to complying with Canadian privacy laws and will respond to requests in accordance with applicable requirements.

39. Australian Privacy Rights

Australian residents have rights under the Privacy Act 1988, including the right to access and correct their personal information. We will handle all requests from Australian residents in accordance with the Australian Privacy Principles.

40. Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach that poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and relevant authorities as required by law. Our breach notification procedures include internal reporting, risk assessment, and notification processes designed to comply with applicable breach notification laws.

Summary of Key Points

  • We collect information to provide and improve our services, with your consent when required
  • You have rights to access, correct, delete, and restrict processing of your data
  • We implement strong security measures to protect your information
  • We may update this policy and will notify you of significant changes
  • Contact us with any privacy-related questions or concerns
